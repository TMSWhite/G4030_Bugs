The Usable Hospital
Why we Designed the System this Way

Who We Are:

  • We are a 200 bed, tertiary care facility, which has been operating at capacity for the last five months.
  • The second and third floors are ICUs.
  • We have five attending MDs on staff at all times, along with a team of nurses, epidemiologists, administrators, and support staff.
  • We are doing are best to cure our patients of any infections (bugs) they may have, but have noticed an increase in Acinetobacter over the last five months.

Why This Site?

  • We needed a convenient way for all our staff to access information about the patients we are caring for, the status of their infections, and any trends that appear over time.
  • We chose to standardize on a relatively-consistent interface so as to reduce training costs and foster communication among different disciplines as they learned to use the system.

What This Site Offers?

  • Customized interfaces for our six classes of users: Patients , Clinicians , Nurses, Epidemiologists, Housekeeping, Administrators.
  • Five consistent ways to view the data: Single Patient, Doctor's List, Floor-Plan, Antibiogram, Temporal Trends.
  • The ability to easily and rapidly get a global view of the data.
    • The Single Patient view shows all of the pertinent information for a patient, and helps the MD choose the most effective and least costly antibiotic.
    • The Doctor's List view lets the clinician see all of their patients, color coded by the severity of their infection; as well as a pertinent summary of their lab tests (their cultures and sensitivity profiles).
    • The Floor-Plan view shows where each of the patients are located, as well as overall totals by floor, room, and for the entire hospital.
      • When looking at a single month (the detail-view), the MD can see the medical record numbers (mrns) for each patient in a room; as well as the list of infections they have, and the severity of the worst infection.
      • The detail-view also lets the nurses see where their patients are located; assess who needs to be isolated or moved; and help balance their workloads.
      • The detail-view also lets housekeepers know which rooms need special cleaning procedures.
      • When looking at the multi-month view (the summary-view), the MD or administrator can see how many infections there were, sorted by floor, bug, and MD; and can assess how bad those infections are.
  • The ablity to filter each view by all relevant variables so as to zoom in on the regions of interest.
    • The user is given the locus of control and the ability to rapidly, reversibly, and incrementally change the type of view, and the variables that contribute to it. Each action causes an immediate update of the right-hand view.
    • Errors are prevented by giving pick-lists; and gracefully caught in the case of the MRN number-entry box by giving informative feedback when invalid MRN numbers are selected.
    • Copious help links are available.
  • The abilty to navigate by scent
    • Hyperlinks at the top of each view allow the user to navigate to neighboring data - e.g. the surrounding months, floors, bugs, and MDs.
    • Hyperlinks connecting the views let the user quickly navigate to related material without having to move the cursor all the way back to the left frame.
  • The ability to get details on demand from each view:
    • From the Single Patient view, you can jump to the Doctor's List, Floor-Plan, and Antibiogram views.
    • From the Doctor's List view, you jump to the Single Patient, and Floor-Plan, views.
    • From the Floor-Plan view, you can jump to the Single Patient views.
  • Shortcuts for experts
    • All of the Fine-Tune pick lists support single key entry; so the user can type the first letter of the desired option, and they simply type the TAB key to have the view refreshed.
    • The Select-A-View dialog puts the view-selection radio buttons next to hyperlinks which jump the cursor to the Fine-Tune filters, thus minimizing motion and maximizing the speed of querying.
  • A comprehensive help page which:
    • helps the user log-in
    • shows them screen-shots of views they can see
    • explains how to navigate among the views
    • provides both user- and view-oriented instructions.

What are we Planning Next?

  • Further customize the Floor-Plan detail-view
    • For nurses - only show patient mrn and severity of illness
      • remove the list of bugs
    • For housekeepers - only show the severity of illness
      • remove the patient mrn and list of bugs
      • change the title and Legend to make it clear that only the rooms in purple and orange require special cleaning.
  • Further customize the Temporal Trends views.
    • Remove the %resistant graph
    • Add a graph representing the minimum sensitivity, divided by bug
  • Implement a generalized time-view interface.
    • Use MySql for dynamic queries, PHP, and the UNIX gd tool to generate GIF images on the fly.
    • Let the user specify the Y axis variable and the variable for multiple lines on a plot.
  • Add linkages to the Multum Drug Database
    • Will allow for emulated on-line ordering of prescriptions, with appropriate spellings, routes, dosages, and intervals of drugs.
    • Will allow for accurate cost comparisons.
